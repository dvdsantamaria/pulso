Thesis

The Gap We're Closing

Quito's fitness market is polarised. There is room for a strength-focused studio that sits between the mass gym and the boutique niche.

The Problem

Smart Fit (9 locations, $20 to $35/mo) offers equipment and low prices but no guidance, no structure, and overcrowded peak hours. CrossFit boxes (Euforia, Naru, $55 to $60/mo) bring community and intensity but coaching quality varies and the format is not for everyone. Premium clubs like Phisique ($80 to $120/mo) and Ecus Fitness ($150/mo) deliver facilities and technology but in large-scale formats that lack personalised coaching.

Our Angle

A strength-focused space that combines structured coaching, small groups (4 to 10), and measurable progression at $75 to $150 per month. Target zones: La Carolina, González Suárez, and Cumbayá, where the 25 to 45 professional segment is dense and underserved in this specific category. The model is built around retention (onboarding, tracking, check-ins) rather than volume.

Product

Strength Training, Systematized

Positioned as a premium alternative to crowded gyms and unstructured classes. Focus on measurable results with guided onboarding, progress tracking, and community.

Value Proposition

  • Efficient training (45 to 60 min) with progressive programming
  • Small groups (4 to 10) with real coach attention
  • Initial assessment, 30-day plan, and regular check-ins
  • Premium experience: scheduling, payments, messaging, community

Operational Differentiators

  • Standardized onboarding: goals, history, measurements, plan
  • Simple tracking: attendance, progress, satisfaction
  • Complementary classes (mobility and balance) for adherence
  • Measurable referral program for organic growth

Product Decision (V1)

Start with a reduced but excellent offering (core strength/functional plus mobility) and expand only when KPIs (retention and margin) justify it. Quality over quantity from day one.

Market

The Quito Fitness Landscape

Quito's fitness market is estimated at USD 40 million and growing. Gym licences quadrupled between 2019 and 2025, but saturation is concentrated in the low-cost segment. The premium, coaching-driven space remains open.

Demand Hypothesis

  • The 25 to 45 professional segment values efficiency and guided training
  • Smart Fit (USD 17M revenue, 2024) proves mass demand exists; PULSO targets the segment willing to pay more for quality
  • 170 new gym licences issued in Quito (Jan to May 2025 alone) confirms accelerating market growth

Key Opportunity

No player in Quito owns the small group strength training format (4 to 6 people, progressive programming, coach-led with measurable progression). CrossFit is the closest analogue but uses a different methodology and larger groups. The Premium Small Group product at $130/mo has no direct competitor and should be positioned as the hero product.

Market Context

The number of gym licences in Quito quadrupled between 2019 and 2025, but saturation is concentrated in the low-cost segment (independent gyms at $35/mo). The premium and boutique segment remains under-served relative to demand, particularly for professionals 25 to 45 who want structured training without the overhead of a wellness club.

Pricing Validation Summary

PULSO's base plan at $75/mo sits 25% above the CrossFit ceiling ($60) and 37% below the wellness club floor ($120). The Plus plan at $110/mo competes directly with Phisique's entry tier but offers smaller groups and more personalised coaching. The Unlimited at $150/mo matches Ecus Fitness (the most expensive gym in Quito) but trades infrastructure for intimacy and individual attention. All price points have real market precedent.

Competitive Moat

PULSO's advantage is not price or facilities. It is the combination of structured strength programming, small groups where the coach knows every member, and a retention system (onboarding, tracking, check-ins) that transforms attendance into measurable results. This is hard to replicate at scale, which is exactly the point.

Customer

Three Core Personas

Our positioning targets individuals seeking consistency, results, and guidance in a premium environment. Three profiles define our product focus.

Persona A: Urban Professional

  • Age: 28 to 45, tight schedule
  • Needs: Train 3x per week, no time wasted, guided workouts
  • Values: Easy booking, punctuality, visible progress

Persona B: Health & Adherence

  • Age: 30 to 55, starting or returning to training
  • Needs: Safety, technique, mobility, less pain
  • Values: Onboarding, gradual progress tracking

Persona C: Performance & Aesthetics

  • Age: 22 to 40, goal-oriented (strength, body recomposition, performance)
  • Needs: Serious programming, measurements, feedback, controlled intensity
  • Values: Solid coaches, progression, block-based plans (4 to 8 weeks)

Commercial Policy

Limited spots per time slot, monthly auto-renewal contracts, discounts only for prepayment (3 or 6 months), no ad-hoc negotiation. Price maintains quality and retention signaling.

Operations

Operational Excellence

Operations designed for consistency: capacity limits, reservations, protocols, cleanliness. The goal is a replicable experience where quality does not depend on individual heroics.

Location & Layout Criteria

  • High foot-traffic zone with safety (target: La Carolina, González Suárez, or Cumbayá)
  • Easy access (car, rideshare, transit) and nearby parking
  • Space: open training area, reception, changing rooms, storage
  • Acoustics and ventilation: critical for classes and comfort

Minimum Tech Stack

  • Class reservations with capacity limits
  • Recurring payments (memberships) and payment links
  • Lead CRM with automated follow-ups
  • Monthly KPI and margin dashboard

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

  • Onboarding: Contact, tour, assessment, enrolment, 30-day plan
  • Class quality: Checklist (punctuality, demo, corrections, close)
  • Retention: Week 2 and 4 check-ins; simple monthly report
  • Hygiene and maintenance: Daily and weekly routines
  • Incidents: Injury, complaints, refunds, rescheduling protocols
Go-to-Market

Customer Acquisition

Measurable go-to-market: content for trust, referrals for efficiency, partnerships for local reach. Paid ads only when funnel and retention are proven.

Funnel (Operational)

  • Automation: reminders, FAQs, social proof

Priority Channels

  • Referrals: Simple incentive per successful enrolment
  • Partnerships: Nutrition, physio, coworking spaces, healthy cafés
  • Ads (Phase 2): Only with tracking and proven offer

Unit Economics

Financial Model

Working with editable assumptions validated against real location costs and market prices in Quito. First goal: achieve break-even with a sustainable member base and strong retention.

CAPEX (Startup Estimate)

  • Fit-out (flooring, paint, lighting, ventilation): $3,000–5,000
  • Equipment (racks, bars, plates, dumbbells, benches, accessories): $6,000–10,000
  • Deposit and first month rent: $2,000–3,000
  • Brand and assets (photos, materials, signage): $800–1,200
  • Systems (booking, payments, CRM setup): $500–800

Estimated total CAPEX: $12,300–20,000 USD

OPEX (Monthly Estimate)

  • Rent, utilities, internet: $1,500–2,200
  • Coach fees and support: $800–1,200
  • Software and payment processing: $100–200
  • Marketing and content: $300–500
  • Cleaning, consumables, maintenance: $200–400

Estimated monthly OPEX: $2,900–4,500 USD

Funding

Initial capital from founders' savings. No external financing or credit. The conservative CAPEX estimate covers essential equipment and space for launch, with reinvestment from revenue as the member base grows. For context: comparable ventures in Quito range from USD 40,000 (basic independent gym) to USD 120,000 (boutique cycling studio). PULSO's lean approach is viable because the model prioritises coaching quality over infrastructure.
